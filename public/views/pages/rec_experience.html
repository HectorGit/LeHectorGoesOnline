Hector is on the third year of his undergraduate degree in Computer Science and Music at the University of Victoria.

He has practical and theoretical knowledge of sound recording, mixing, and editing. Hector is interested in the theory behind the creation of audio effects, and in digital signal processing.

Hector has:

  • Planned and executed successful recording sessions

  • Collaborated with other creative artists

  • Prepared to solve technical issues promptly


  • Adjusted audio files as needed

  • Developed good relationships with collaborators

  • Ensured equipment proper and safe use


Hector learned about stereo recording techniques from Kirk McNally. With McNally, Hector practiced using different microphone configurations such as ORTF and the MS (mid-side) technique.

In the summer of 2014, in Guatemala, Hector completed an internship with recognized recording artist Jorge Estrada Garavito.

Hector has learned about different audio effects one can use to increase the quality of recordings, such as equalizers, filters, compressors, etc. He has also learned in the computer music classes about digital audio (from Andrew Schloss) and signal processing (from Peter Driessen).

Hector is currently learning about music information retrieval with George Tzanetakis. In this course, students learn about audio features, and how they can be extracted with signal processing and machine learning techniques.

Hector has recently contributed to two short films - 'Piano Girl', and 'Knot For Sale'. He recorded audio for these as well as created a musical score for them.

Hector is a Member of the CMCU - Students union for the Combined Computer Science and Music Program. He has collaborated with the organization of "Track Day" activities, where a song by a local band is recorded, and mixed.
